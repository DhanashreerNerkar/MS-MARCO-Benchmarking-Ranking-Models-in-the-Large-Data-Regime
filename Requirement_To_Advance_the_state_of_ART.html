REQUIREMENTS TO ADVANCE THE STATE OF THE ART

This section outlines the steps required to make valid and useful contributions to ad hoc ranking research. We examine both historical successes and modern challenges in establishing truly impactful improvements.

To advance information retrieval research meaningfully, we need contributions that are both valid because we can confidently verify they represent genuine improvements, and useful because these improvements can be easily deployed across diverse real-world applications. This section establishes a framework for evaluating research contributions against these dual criteria, drawing lessons from both the successful development of BM25 in the 1990s and the current era of BERT-style neural rankers trained on MS MARCO data.

The Validity and Usefulness Framework

Robust Usefulness
Easy deployment across many real-world applications with reliable performance
External Validity
Findings hold up when data distribution changes slightly - different domains, queries, or judging schemes
Internal Validity
Statistical and mathematical correctness - results are reliable and not due to chance or multiple testing

Historical Success vs Modern Challenges

1990s TREC Era
BM25 Development
  • Small training data regime (50 query topics)
  • Varied document lengths and writing styles
  • Robust to document length variations
  • Widely deployed in real systems
  • 2,420+ citations for "Okapi at TREC-3"
  • Strong external validity across domains
2019+ MS MARCO Era
BERT-style Rankers
  • Large training data regime (100K+ queries)
  • Hundreds of thousands of training examples
  • Dramatic effectiveness improvements
  • Computational complexity challenges
  • Deployment requires significant expertise
  • External validity still being established

Current Research Challenges

🔄
Multiple Testing
Public leaderboards allow repeated submissions, risking overfitting to test sets through statistical fishing
📏
IR Metrics Scale
Questions about whether metrics like MRR and NDCG are on interval scales, affecting statistical validity
🚀
Deployment Gap
Neural rankers require PhD-level expertise for tuning and deployment, limiting practical adoption
🌐
Domain Transfer
Unclear if improvements on MS MARCO generalize to other domains without extensive retraining

Critical Research Questions

Is the "SOTA" progress meaningful?
Are leaderboard improvements representing genuine advances or just metric optimization?
Is MRR a good metric for evaluation?
Questions about interval scales and whether means of IR metrics are statistically valid
Are all top runs effectively tied?
Has the leaderboard reached saturation where differences are no longer meaningful?
Are we seeing multiple submission overfitting?
Do teams benefit unfairly from repeated submissions and parameter tuning on test sets?
Do findings hold with slight data changes?
Testing external validity through TREC Deep Learning Track with different judging schemes
Is there a standard deployment playbook?
Can these techniques be easily deployed without requiring specialized expertise?
